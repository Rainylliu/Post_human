Goal

To create a comprehensive database that documents and tracks the genetic changes in flora and fauna resulting from exposure to various levels of nuclear radiation, including extreme scenarios like those following a nuclear war.

Solution

Establish a global genetic repository to track and analyze genetic changes in flora and fauna following nuclear disasters. This will enhance understanding of biological adaptation in high-radiation environments, guide conservation and restoration projects, inform environmental policies, and serve as a valuable resource. It's a strategic approach to managing biodiversity and ecosystem recovery in post-nuclear contexts.

Significance

This understanding is crucial for guiding future efforts in environmental restoration and ecosystem reconstruction. In addition to aiding in the monitoring of changes in biodiversity, this global genetic repository would serve as a valuable resource in formulating plans for ecological conservation and sustainable development.

Approach

Gathering genetic samples from a wide array of species across various radiation-affected regions. Employing advanced genomic sequencing and bioinformatics for data analysis. Comparing post-disaster genetic data with pre-disaster data to identify mutations and adaptations. Working with international partners for a comprehensive data collection encompassing diverse ecosystems.
